Good design isn't only beautiful — it solves meaningful problems, simplifies complexity, and creates measurable business value.

Since 2021, I've worked at the intersection of business strategy and user experience — first finding my footing in product design, and now leading UI/UX efforts as an Assistant Manager at EY GDS in Bangalore.

My work spans the full product lifecycle: research, interaction design, product strategy, wireframing, prototyping, design systems, visual design, accessibility, and everything in between. I've partnered with clients, product owners, business teams, developers, and QA to take ideas from a rough sketch to a shipped, working product — and I've learned that the best solutions come from staying close to that whole process, not just the parts that are easy to show in a portfolio.

What drives me is the harder half of design: taking something genuinely complicated — a financial workflow, an enterprise dashboard, an AI-powered tool — and making it feel obvious. I believe in design that's user-centred by default and improved through iteration, not perfected in a single pass. And I've come to see developer handoff and implementation support as part of the design job, not the end of it — a product isn't well-designed until it's well-built.

Selected Work

Six products, six different kinds of complexity.

Enterprise dashboards, AI-assisted workflows, and consumer fintech — each project below started as a hard problem before it became a good interface.

PROJECT 01

Neo CFO

Enterprise FinTech Dashboard Design

A complete UI/UX overhaul of an enterprise financial platform used by finance teams to manage complex reporting, forecasting, and analysis workflows.

Problem

Finance teams worked across fragmented tools and dense spreadsheets, with data buried across disconnected views. Getting a clear read on the numbers took far longer than it should.

Approach

Restructured the information architecture around how finance teams actually think — by workflow, not feature — and designed a dashboard system built for dense data, multiple roles, and high-stakes decisions.

Outcome

A cohesive, enterprise-grade platform where forecasting, reporting, and analysis feel structured and navigable instead of overwhelming.

Impact

Finance teams could move from raw data to a decision in far fewer steps, and the dashboard system became the template for how the product visualises data going forward.

PROJECT 02

F Index Software

Product Design Design Systems

End-to-end product design for F Index Software, from early research through to a shipped design system and production UI.

Problem

The product needed a design foundation that could scale — one that didn't exist yet — while features kept shipping on a live roadmap.

Approach

Ran user research to ground the design in real usage patterns, then moved through wireframes into a full UI language and a reusable design system, handing off components that mapped directly to how the product was actually being built.

Outcome

A consistent design system and production UI that let the team design and build faster, with far less duplicated effort between design and engineering.

Impact

New features could be designed against an existing system rather than a blank canvas, shortening the distance between concept and release.

PROJECT 03

Spark AI

AI Product Interface Design

Interface design for Spark AI, a platform built around AI-assisted workflows and prompt-based interactions.

Problem

AI-powered features are only useful if people trust them and know how to work with them. Prompting needed to feel natural, not like operating a black box.

Approach

Designed the prompt experience around clarity and control — showing people what the AI was doing, giving them ways to guide and refine results, and keeping the interface out of the way once a workflow was running.

Outcome

A modern, focused interface where AI-assisted workflows are fast to start and easy to trust.

Impact

Users completed AI-assisted tasks with noticeably less friction, and the prompt interaction patterns became a reference point for other AI features on the team.

PROJECT 04

NAI Prompt Generator

AI Productivity

Product design for an AI productivity tool built to help people write and manage better prompts.

Problem

Writing effective prompts was a skill most users hadn't developed yet, and managing a growing library of prompts was becoming its own chore.

Approach

Simplified the experience down to what mattered: fast prompt creation, clear organisation, and lightweight guidance that improved prompts without a learning curve.

Outcome

A focused tool where creating, refining, and reusing prompts takes a fraction of the effort it used to.

Impact

The simplified workflow made the tool approachable for first-time users while still holding up for people managing large prompt libraries.

PROJECT 05

Maiven

Enterprise AI Platform

Product design for Maiven, an AI-powered software platform built for enterprise workflows.

Problem

The product needed to support enterprise-scale workflows without the interface buckling under the weight of its own features.

Approach

Mapped how the platform needed to scale before designing a single screen, then built a UI that could hold enterprise complexity while staying clear to a first-time user.

Outcome

A scalable UI that supports genuinely complex enterprise workflows without sacrificing clarity for people new to the platform.

Impact

The platform could grow into new enterprise use cases without needing a redesign at every step, because the interface was built to scale from day one.

PROJECT 06

Slice Credit Card

FinTech Mobile

Product design for Slice, a mobile-first credit card experience built around everyday financial trust.

Problem

Credit products live or die on trust, especially on mobile, where one confusing moment can make people abandon a financial decision entirely.

Approach

Designed the experience mobile-first from the ground up, prioritising clarity at every touchpoint. Security and trust were built into the visual language and interaction design, alongside a deliberately simple UI.

Outcome

A mobile experience where managing a credit card feels straightforward and secure, without the usual friction of financial products.

Impact

A simpler, more trustworthy financial experience that made everyday card management something users felt confident doing on their own.
